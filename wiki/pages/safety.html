Part Safety

We use DH5-alpha competent cells, BL21DE3 competent cells, and BY4741 yeast strain for our experiments this year. Before disposal, we thoroughly autoclave or bleach E.coli plates, yeast plates, tips, and tubes. The cloned pGal1,10-IsPETase plasmids can only be transformed into yeast and bacteria since the plasmids only have 2μ ORI for yeast DNA replication and ORI for bacterial DNA replication. Bacteria and yeast will not pick up plasmids without specific selection on the plates. The cloned T7-IsPETase plasmids can only be transformed into bacteria since the plasmids only have ORI for bacterial DNA replication. Bacteria will not pick up plasmids without specific selection on the plates.

Figure 1: Autoclave



Our team is using traditional enzyme digestion cloning strategies to clone IsPETase downstream of pGal1,10 promoter and T7 promoter. Once our team makes pGal1,10-IsPETase plasmids and T7-IsPETase plasmids, the team will use a site-directed mutagenesis kit to create pGal1,10-IsPETaseS121E /D186H/R280A plasmids and T7-IsPETaseS121E/D186H/R280A plasmids. Those two promoters are inducible. pGal1,10 promoter is induced in the presence of galactose, and the T7 promoter is induced in the presence of IPTG(Isopropyl β-D-1-thiogalactopyranoside). The parts our team created will not be able to express without the inducible compounds. Also, the function of IsPETase is to degrade PET plastic, not a toxic protein, even if it’s expressed after being induced.

Lab Safety

We do not plan to do any activity prohibited in the iGEM competition, and all our work is covered by the White list. In our experiment, we only use permitted yeast and bacteria, no other organisms or hazardous chemicals are used in the project. We also do not plan to use any artificial intelligence in the project.

In Kang Chiao International School Xiugang Campus, we use a level 1 standard microbiological lab consisting of an open bench, waste containers, and a chemical fume hood. There is a chemical waste container that separates organic and inorganic waste. Moreover, there are separate biohazard waste containers, one for bacteria and yeast plates, and another for tips and Eppendorf tubes, to avoid risks of bio-waste contamination. In addition, the Esco Airstream Laminar hood in the laboratory helps us avoid contamination during bacteria and yeast inoculation.

Safety Training


All of our team members received safety training, including familiarization with lab access procedures, microbial techniques, personal biosecurity, biosafety equipment usage, and emergency protocols. We ensure that all the team members undertake experiments safely and responsibly. We also implemented biosafety and biosecurity measures, including medical surveillance, data access controls, and the use of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) to mitigate risks. The team is also supervised by Ms. Kuo, an experienced researcher and teacher in the field of microbiology, when handling organisms and other chemicals. In addition, iGEM ambassador Sabrina Yao and three university labs checked that the KCIS laboratory meets all the safety requirements.
